\begin{abstract}
  大规模结构观测中的宇宙学参数推断依赖大量模拟样本估计协方差矩阵，并需要同时刻画暗物质密度场和速度场。传统 N 体模拟精度高但计算代价昂贵，基于拉格朗日微扰论的快速方法计算效率高，却难以恢复非线性小尺度结构和速度场细节。针对这一问题，本文提出一种数据驱动的宇宙学快速模拟流程，以 Zel'dovich 近似（ZA）提供位移场和速度场的大尺度基准，再使用两个三维生成对抗残差网络分别学习位移残差和速度残差。位移网络以 ZA 位移场为条件输入；速度网络以 ZA 速度场和位移网络推理得到的最终位移场为联合条件输入，从而利用已恢复的非线性结构信息补全速度场。网络采用三维 U 形生成器和多尺度块级判别器，并结合分块推理、位移残差高通滤波和速度场旋转平均策略，使方法能够扩展到完整模拟盒子。

  本文使用 Quijote 低精度 N 体模拟验证该流程。结果表明，与 ZA、2LPT 和 ALPT 等微扰论基准相比，新流程在暗物质密度场的切片形态、残差概率分布函数、功率谱和双谱上均更接近 N 体参考结果，能够显著恢复小尺度非线性功率和高阶相关。功率谱协方差矩阵的整体结构也与 N 体结果较为一致，但小尺度对角方差仍存在一定低估，且当前估计受样本数量限制。速度场方面，新流程在速度模切片、速度散度功率谱和各速度分量功率谱上相较 ZA 有明显改进，但对旋度和维里化区域中的小尺度随机速度仍恢复不足。总体而言，本文方法在保持大尺度结构一致性的同时改善了密度场和速度场的小尺度统计性质，为面向多探针宇宙学分析的快速模拟提供了可行方案。

\end{abstract}

\begin{abstract*}
  Cosmological parameter inference from large-scale structure observations requires many simulation realizations for covariance estimation, together with accurate dark matter density and velocity fields. Full N-body simulations are accurate but expensive, while fast perturbative methods are efficient but insufficient on nonlinear small scales. This work develops a data-driven fast simulation pipeline based on scale separation. The Zel'dovich approximation (ZA) provides the large-scale displacement and velocity baselines, and two three-dimensional generative adversarial residual networks learn the displacement and velocity corrections. The displacement network is conditioned on the ZA displacement field, while the velocity network is conditioned on both the ZA velocity field and the final displacement field inferred by the displacement network. A three-dimensional U-shaped generator, multi-scale patch discriminators, tiled inference, high-pass filtering of displacement residuals, and rotation averaging for velocity inference are combined so that the method can be applied to full simulation boxes.

  The pipeline is validated with low-resolution N-body simulations from the Quijote suite. Compared with ZA, 2LPT, and ALPT, the proposed method produces density fields that are closer to the N-body reference in visual slices, residual probability density functions, power spectra, and bispectra, indicating improved recovery of nonlinear power and higher-order correlations. The matter power-spectrum covariance matrix is also broadly consistent with the N-body result, although the small-scale diagonal variance remains underestimated and the estimate is limited by the number of realizations. For the velocity field, the method improves velocity-magnitude slices, velocity-divergence power spectra, and component-wise power spectra relative to ZA, but vorticity and stochastic velocities in virialized regions are still under-recovered. Overall, the method improves small-scale density and velocity statistics while preserving large-scale consistency, providing a viable route toward fast simulations for multi-probe cosmological analyses.
